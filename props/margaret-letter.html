Recovered Effects

Command tent, North Star site — personal correspondence, one (1) page
Dear Theodore, I hope the weather up there is kinder than the papers say. Your father sends his regards and wants to know if you received the wool socks. We expect five of your cousins to visit as guests this summer, most likely before the fair. Aunt Ruth says August is best for the garden, though I disagree. I attended vesper service at the chapel on Sunday, and Father Malone confirms the roof will be repaired by fall. Please hold on to the item you promised your brother, the little carved bird, until you are home again. The relief of seeing you at the door will be worth every week. Write when the mail arrives, if it ever does out there. With love, Margaret
A folded letter, soft at the creases from rereading. Nothing remarkable about it.
